Our Story

A passion for colour, craft and Indian textiles

Our Journey

Over 20 Years of Beautiful Textiles

Dream of Olwen has been a cherished part of Bath's Guildhall Market for over two decades — the first stall you see as you step in from the High Street, instantly recognisable by its explosion of colour and pattern. What started as a passion for Indian textiles has grown into one of the market's most distinctive and loved stalls.

We travel regularly to India, visiting artisan workshops and cooperatives across the great textile heartlands — the block printers of Sanganer near Jaipur, the silk weavers of Varanasi, the Sozni and Aari embroiderers of Kashmir, and the mirror-work artisans of Gujarat's Kutch region. Each item carries the skill and tradition of generations of craftspeople.

Our Values

Direct from Artisans to Bath

We believe in fair, direct trade with the craftspeople who create our textiles. By buying directly from workshops and cooperatives in Rajasthan, Kashmir, Gujarat, Varanasi and Bengal, we ensure that artisans receive fair payment for their extraordinary skill — and you receive pieces of genuine quality and heritage.

Every Sanganeri block-print scarf, Kashmiri pashmina, Bagru-dyed bed sheet and Kantha-stitched cushion cover has been personally selected for its quality, beauty and authenticity. We don't do mass-produced — each piece is unique, carrying the slight variations that mark true handcraft.
